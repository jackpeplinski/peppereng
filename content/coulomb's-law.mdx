- $F =$ Electrostatic force
- $|q1|$, $|q2| =$ magnitude of two charges
- $r^2 = $ distance between the two charges
- <InlineMath math={CommonMathData.coulombsConstantKApprox}/>, Coulomb's Constant

# Problems
<Dropdown 
  title={["Two particles with equal charge experience a force of 10 nN when they are 15 cm equidistant to each other. What is the magnitude of the charge on each particle?"]} 
  body={[
    <InlineMath math="\text{Diagram:}"/>,
    <img src={CoulombsLaw1}/>,
    "Equidistant means equal distance from each other. ",
    "Therefore, the charges are 15 cm from the midpoint between them and a total of 30 cm away from each other.",
    <Divider/>,
    <InlineMath math="\text{Solution:}"/>,
    <BlockMath math={CommonMathData.coulombsLaw}/>,
    <BlockMath math="\text{because the charges are equal }|q_1|=|q_2|=q"/>,
    <BlockMath math="\overrightarrow{F} =k\frac{q^2}{r^2}"/>,
    <BlockMath math="\sqrt{\frac{\overrightarrow{F}r^2}{k}}=q"/>,
    <BlockMath math="\sqrt{\frac{{10\times10^{-19}}(30\times10^{-2})^2}{8.9\times10^9}}=q"/>,
    <BlockMath math="\boxed{-3.2\times10^{-10} \text{C} = q}"/>,
  ]}
/>

<Dropdown 
  title="If you double the distance between two point charges, by which factor does the force between the particles change?"
  body={[
    <BlockMath math={"\\text{The force is given by: }"+CommonMathData.coulombsLaw}/>,
    <BlockMath math="\text{Increasing the distance will affect the force, we can label this affect as } x \text{ below.}"/>,
    <BlockMath math="x\overrightarrow{F} =k\frac{|q_1||q_2|}{(2r)^2}"/>,
    <BlockMath math="x\overrightarrow{F} =k\frac{|q_1||q_2|}{4(r^2)}"/>,
    <BlockMath math="x\overrightarrow{F} =\frac{1}{4}\underbrace{k\frac{|q_1||q_2|}{r^2}}_{\overrightarrow{F}}"/>,
    <BlockMath math="x\space\cancel{\overrightarrow{F}} = \frac{1}{4}\cancel{\overrightarrow{F}}"/>,
    <BlockMath math="x=\frac{1}{4}"/>,
    <BlockMath math="\boxed{\text{Doubling the distance decreases the force between the particles by a factor of }\frac{1}{4}.}"/>,
  ]}
/>
