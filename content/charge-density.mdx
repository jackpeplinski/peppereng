The charge density on an object can be determined by the dimension of the object:

| Symbol | Description            | Formula | Units |
|--------|------------------------|---------|-------|
|$\lambda$  | (1D) linear charge density  |<InlineMath math="\frac{q}{\text{length}}"/>       |<InlineMath math="\frac{\text{C}}{\text{m}}"/>         |
|$\sigma$   | (2D) surface charge density |<InlineMath math="\frac{q}{\text{area}}"/>       |<InlineMath math="\frac{ \text{C} } { \text{m}^{2}}"/> |
|$\rho$     | (3D) volume charge density  |<InlineMath math="\frac{q}{\text{volume}}"/>      |<InlineMath math="\frac{ \text{C} } { \text{m}^{3} }"/>      |
