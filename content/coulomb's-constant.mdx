$$
k = \frac1{4\pi\epsilon_0}
$$

- $k$ is a special constant used in multiple formulas. $k$ is often written instead of $\frac1{4\pi\epsilon_0}$ because it is used frequently.
- $k ≈ 8.988\times10^9 \frac{Nm^2}{C^2}$
- $\epsilon_0 ≈ 8.8541\times10^-12 \frac{F}{m}$, vacuum permittivity, permittivity of free space, or electric constant
- $\epsilon_0$ pronounced 'epsilon naught'.
